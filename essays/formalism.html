Potential Applications of Russian Formalism in CG Text

November 11, 2016
Ulysses Foote-McNabb

Computers have long been able to generate visual art, and slightly more recently have become able to generate readable English. The issue with all art generated by computers is, simply, that it lacks feeling. In the early 20th century, there was a movement in Russia called "Formalism." The leaders of the movement argued that we could develop a scientific understanding of techniques in literature.

Russian Formalism is a school of literary criticism that seeks a scientific understanding of the effects of literature on readers. Rejecting subjectivism, the Formalists believed that certain aspects of literature had predictable effects on readers.
